Sustainable Management of Natural Resources

ਕੁਦਰਤੀ ਸਰੋਤਾਂ ਦਾ ਟਿਕਾਊ ਪ੍ਰਬੰਧਨ

Reading Corner ਪੜ੍ਹੋ · Story Mode

ਸਾਡੀ ਸਾਂਝੀ ਦੌਲਤOur Shared Wealth

ਜੰਗਲ, ਪਾਣੀ, ਕੋਲਾ ਤੇ ਪੈਟਰੋਲੀਅਮ ਸਾਡੇ ਕੁਦਰਤੀ ਸਾਧਨ ਹਨ, ਜੋ ਸਾਰਿਆਂ ਦੀ ਸਾਂਝੀ ਦੌਲਤ ਹਨ। ਇਨ੍ਹਾਂ ਦੀ ਸੋਚ-ਸਮਝ ਕੇ ਵਰਤੋਂ ਨੂੰ ਟਿਕਾਊ ਪ੍ਰਬੰਧਨ (sustainable management) ਕਹਿੰਦੇ ਹਨ — ਭਾਵ ਇੰਨੀ ਵਰਤੋਂ ਕਿ ਸਾਡੀਆਂ ਲੋੜਾਂ ਵੀ ਪੂਰੀਆਂ ਹੋਣ ਤੇ ਆਉਣ ਵਾਲੀਆਂ ਪੀੜ੍ਹੀਆਂ ਲਈ ਵੀ ਬਚੇ। ਇਸ ਦਾ ਸਾਰ ਤਿੰਨ R ਵਿੱਚ ਹੈ: Reduce (ਘਟਾਓ), Reuse (ਮੁੜ ਵਰਤੋ), ਤੇ Recycle (ਪੁਨਰ-ਚਕਰਨ)। ਥੋੜ੍ਹੀ ਵਰਤੋਂ ਹੀ ਸਭ ਤੋਂ ਵੱਡੀ ਬਚਤ ਹੈ।

Forests, water, coal and petroleum are our natural resources — wealth shared by everyone. Using them wisely is called sustainable management: using them enough to meet our needs while leaving some for future generations. Its essence is the three R's: Reduce, Reuse and Recycle. Using less is the greatest saving of all.

ਲੋਕਾਂ ਦੇ ਜੰਗਲThe People's Forests

ਜੰਗਲ ਸਿਰਫ਼ ਰੁੱਖ ਨਹੀਂ; ਇਹ ਲੱਖਾਂ ਜੀਵਾਂ ਦੇ ਘਰ ਤੇ ਨੇੜਲੇ ਲੋਕਾਂ ਦੀ ਰੋਜ਼ੀ ਹਨ। ਇਸ ਲਈ ਜੰਗਲਾਂ ਦੀ ਸੰਭਾਲ ਵਿੱਚ ਸਥਾਨਕ ਭਾਈਚਾਰੇ ਦੀ ਭਾਗੀਦਾਰੀ ਜ਼ਰੂਰੀ ਹੈ। ਚਿਪਕੋ ਅੰਦੋਲਨ ਵਿੱਚ ਪਿੰਡਾਂ ਦੇ ਲੋਕ, ਖ਼ਾਸ ਕਰ ਔਰਤਾਂ, ਰੁੱਖਾਂ ਨੂੰ ਜੱਫੀ ਪਾ ਕੇ ਉਨ੍ਹਾਂ ਨੂੰ ਕੱਟਣ ਤੋਂ ਬਚਾਉਂਦੇ ਸਨ। ਰਾਜਸਥਾਨ ਦੀ ਬਿਸ਼ਨੋਈ ਬਰਾਦਰੀ ਸਦੀਆਂ ਤੋਂ ਰੁੱਖਾਂ ਤੇ ਜੀਵਾਂ ਦੀ ਰੱਖਿਆ ਕਰਦੀ ਆਈ ਹੈ। ਇਹ ਦਿਖਾਉਂਦਾ ਹੈ ਕਿ ਸਥਾਨਕ ਲੋਕ ਜੰਗਲ ਦੇ ਸਭ ਤੋਂ ਵਧੀਆ ਰਾਖੇ ਹਨ।

A forest is more than trees; it is home to millions of creatures and a livelihood for nearby people. So conserving forests needs the participation of local communities. In the Chipko movement, villagers — especially women — hugged trees to stop them being felled. The Bishnoi community of Rajasthan has protected trees and animals for centuries. This shows that local people are often the best guardians of a forest.

ਮੀਂਹ ਦੀ ਹਰ ਬੂੰਦEvery Drop of Rain

ਪਾਣੀ ਜੀਵਨ ਹੈ, ਪਰ ਸਾਫ਼ ਪਾਣੀ ਸੀਮਿਤ ਹੈ। ਸਦੀਆਂ ਤੋਂ ਭਾਰਤ ਦੇ ਲੋਕ ਪਾਣੀ ਸੰਭਾਲ ਲਈ ਸਿਆਣੇ ਢੰਗ ਵਰਤਦੇ ਆਏ ਹਨ। ਵੱਡੇ ਬੰਨ੍ਹ ਬਿਜਲੀ ਤੇ ਸਿੰਚਾਈ ਦਿੰਦੇ ਹਨ, ਪਰ ਇਹ ਲੋਕਾਂ ਨੂੰ ਉਜਾੜਦੇ ਵੀ ਹਨ ਤੇ ਵਾਤਾਵਰਨ ਨੂੰ ਨੁਕਸਾਨ ਪਹੁੰਚਾਉਂਦੇ ਹਨ। ਇਸ ਦੇ ਮੁਕਾਬਲੇ ਮੀਂਹ ਦੇ ਪਾਣੀ ਦੀ ਸੰਭਾਲ ਤੇ ਛੋਟੇ ਤਲਾਬ ਧਰਤੀ ਹੇਠਲੇ ਪਾਣੀ ਨੂੰ ਭਰਦੇ ਹਨ ਤੇ ਪਿੰਡ ਦੇ ਕਾਬੂ ਵਿੱਚ ਰਹਿੰਦੇ ਹਨ। ਹਰ ਬੂੰਦ ਬਚਾਉਣਾ ਹੀ ਭਵਿੱਖ ਬਚਾਉਣਾ ਹੈ।

Water is life, but clean water is limited. For centuries the people of India have used clever methods of water harvesting. Large dams provide electricity and irrigation, but they also displace people and harm the environment. In contrast, rainwater harvesting and small local ponds recharge the groundwater and stay under village control. Saving every drop is saving the future.

Why Manage Natural Resources?

Natural resources such as forests, water, coal and petroleum are limited or easily damaged. Sustainable management means using them wisely so present and future generations both benefit.

Use resources without exhausting them. (ਸਰੋਤ ਖਤਮ ਕੀਤੇ ਬਿਨਾਂ ਵਰਤੋਂ।)

Sustainable Development

Sustainable development meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their needs.

Short-term viewSustainable view
Extract as much as possible now.Use only what can be renewed or replaced.
Pollution treated as someone else's problem.Pollution control is part of planning.
Profit only.People + environment + economy.

Forests and Wildlife

Forests provide timber, fuel, fodder, medicines, oxygen, rainfall support, soil protection and habitat for wildlife.

  • They maintain biodiversity.
  • They prevent soil erosion and floods.
  • They support local communities and tribal livelihoods.

Stakeholders in Forest Conservation

StakeholderInterest
Local peopleFuelwood, fodder, fruits, small forest products.
Forest departmentControls and manages forest land.
IndustrialistsTimber, paper pulp, raw materials.
Wildlife enthusiastsBiodiversity and animal protection.
Local communities must be included; they depend on forests daily.

Chipko Movement

The Chipko movement began in the Himalayan region when villagers hugged trees to stop contractors from cutting them.

  • It showed the power of community participation.
  • It linked forest conservation with local livelihood.
  • It reminded planners that forests are more than timber.

People's Participation

Conservation succeeds when people who use resources help manage them.

Without participationWith participation
Rules are ignored or resisted.Rules match local needs.
Short-term extraction dominates.Long-term benefit is protected.
Conflict increases.Responsibility is shared.

Dams: Benefits

  • Store water for irrigation and drinking.
  • Generate hydroelectricity.
  • Help control floods when managed well.
  • Support industry and cities.
Dams are multipurpose projects, but every benefit has a cost.

Dams: Problems

ProblemExplanation
SocialVillages and farms may be submerged; people displaced.
EnvironmentalForests and wildlife habitats are destroyed.
EconomicHuge construction cost; siltation reduces life.

Water Harvesting

Water harvesting captures rainwater where it falls and allows it to recharge groundwater.

  • Reduces runoff and soil erosion.
  • Improves water availability in dry months.
  • Uses local geography and community labour.

Watershed Management

A watershed is an area that drains water into a common point. Watershed management conserves soil and water together.

1. Plant vegetation on slopes.
2. Build contour bunds and check dams.
3. Slow down runoff.
4. Recharge groundwater.
5. Improve crop and drinking water supply.

Coal and Petroleum

Coal and petroleum are fossil fuels formed over millions of years. Their reserves are limited, so they must be conserved.

  • Coal is used in thermal power plants and industries.
  • Petroleum gives petrol, diesel, kerosene, LPG and petrochemicals.
  • Burning both releases air pollutants and CO₂.

Why Conserve Coal & Petroleum?

ReasonExplanation
Finite supplyThey cannot be renewed quickly.
PollutionBurning releases smoke, SO₂, NOx and CO₂.
Economic valueThey are raw materials for many industries.
Future generationsSaving them supports long-term energy security.

Cleaner Choices

  • Use public transport, cycling or walking when possible.
  • Switch off lights, fans and appliances when not in use.
  • Use efficient bulbs and appliances.
  • Adopt solar, biogas and other renewables where practical.
  • Maintain vehicles to reduce fuel waste and smoke.

Energy, Economy and Equity

Sustainable management is also about fairness. Resources should not be consumed by a few while pollution and scarcity harm others.

A good policy balances ecological protection, economic development and social justice.

Biodiversity Loss

Biodiversity decreases when habitats are destroyed or overused.

CauseEffect
DeforestationHabitat loss, soil erosion, climate effects.
PollutionWater/air toxicity harms species.
OverexploitationPopulation decline and extinction risk.

Conservation Strategies

  • Protected areas: national parks, sanctuaries and biosphere reserves.
  • Afforestation and reforestation.
  • Sustainable harvest limits.
  • Community forest management.
  • Reduce demand for products that drive habitat loss.

Case Study: Local Resource Conflict

A forest village needs firewood, an industry wants timber, and wildlife needs habitat. A sustainable solution would:

  • Give local people controlled access to minor forest produce.
  • Limit commercial cutting and require replanting.
  • Protect core wildlife zones.
  • Provide alternatives such as LPG/biogas and efficient stoves.

Exam Writing Frame

For "How can an individual contribute to sustainable management?" write:

  1. Follow the 5 R's.
  2. Save water and electricity.
  3. Use public transport or carpool.
  4. Plant and protect trees.
  5. Avoid wasteful consumption and single-use plastic.

Spot the Error

Error 1: Recycling is always the first step.
Fix: Refuse and Reduce come before recycling.
Error 2: Dams have only benefits.
Fix: They also cause social, environmental and economic problems.
Error 3: Forests are useful only for wood.
Fix: They protect soil, water, climate, biodiversity and livelihoods.

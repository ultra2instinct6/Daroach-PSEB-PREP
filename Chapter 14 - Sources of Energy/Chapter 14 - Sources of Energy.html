ਚੰਗਾ ਬਾਲਣ ਕੀ ਹੁੰਦਾ ਹੈ?What Makes a Good Fuel?

ਹਰ ਮਸ਼ੀਨ ਤੇ ਸਰੀਰ ਨੂੰ ਚੱਲਣ ਲਈ ਊਰਜਾ ਚਾਹੀਦੀ ਹੈ, ਪਰ ਹਰ ਊਰਜਾ ਸਰੋਤ ਬਰਾਬਰ ਨਹੀਂ ਹੁੰਦਾ। ਇੱਕ ਚੰਗਾ ਬਾਲਣ ਬਹੁਤ ਸਾਰੀ ਗਰਮੀ ਦਿੰਦਾ ਹੈ (ਉੱਚ ਕੈਲੋਰੀਮਾਨ), ਸੌਖਾ ਮਿਲਦਾ ਹੈ, ਸਸਤਾ ਹੁੰਦਾ ਹੈ, ਸੰਭਾਲਣਾ ਸੁਰੱਖਿਅਤ ਹੁੰਦਾ ਹੈ ਤੇ ਬਲਣ ਵੇਲੇ ਘੱਟ ਧੂੰਆਂ ਤੇ ਸੁਆਹ ਛੱਡਦਾ ਹੈ। ਕੋਈ ਵੀ ਬਾਲਣ ਸੰਪੂਰਨ ਨਹੀਂ ਹੁੰਦਾ, ਇਸ ਲਈ ਸਾਨੂੰ ਲੋੜ ਅਨੁਸਾਰ ਸਭ ਤੋਂ ਵਧੀਆ ਚੋਣ ਕਰਨੀ ਪੈਂਦੀ ਹੈ ਤੇ ਪ੍ਰਦੂਸ਼ਣ ਬਾਰੇ ਵੀ ਸੋਚਣਾ ਪੈਂਦਾ ਹੈ।

Every machine and body needs energy to run, but not every source of energy is equal. A good fuel gives out a lot of heat (a high calorific value), is easy to get, is cheap, is safe to store, and leaves little smoke and ash when it burns. No fuel is perfect, so we must choose the best one for each need while also thinking about the pollution it causes.

ਸੂਰਜ ਦਾ ਤੋਹਫ਼ਾThe Sun's Gift

ਸੂਰਜ ਧਰਤੀ ਦੀ ਲਗਭਗ ਸਾਰੀ ਊਰਜਾ ਦਾ ਮੂਲ ਸਰੋਤ ਹੈ। ਇਹ ਇੱਕ ਨਵਿਆਉਣਯੋਗ (renewable) ਸਰੋਤ ਹੈ ਜੋ ਅਰਬਾਂ ਸਾਲ ਚੱਲਦਾ ਰਹੇਗਾ। ਸੋਲਰ ਸੈੱਲ ਸੂਰਜ ਦੀ ਰੋਸ਼ਨੀ ਨੂੰ ਸਿੱਧੇ ਬਿਜਲੀ ਵਿੱਚ ਬਦਲਦੇ ਹਨ, ਤੇ ਸੋਲਰ ਕੁੱਕਰ ਤੇ ਵਾਟਰ ਹੀਟਰ ਇਸ ਦੀ ਗਰਮੀ ਵਰਤਦੇ ਹਨ। ਪੌਣ ਊਰਜਾ, ਪਾਣੀ ਊਰਜਾ ਤੇ ਬਾਇਓਮਾਸ ਵੀ ਅਸਲ ਵਿੱਚ ਸੂਰਜੀ ਊਰਜਾ ਦੇ ਹੀ ਰੂਪ ਹਨ। ਪਥਰਾਟ ਬਾਲਣਾਂ ਦੇ ਉਲਟ, ਇਹ ਸਰੋਤ ਖ਼ਤਮ ਨਹੀਂ ਹੁੰਦੇ ਤੇ ਵਾਤਾਵਰਨ ਨੂੰ ਘੱਟ ਪ੍ਰਦੂਸ਼ਿਤ ਕਰਦੇ ਹਨ।

The Sun is the ultimate source of almost all of Earth's energy. It is a renewable source that will keep shining for billions of years. Solar cells turn sunlight directly into electricity, while solar cookers and water heaters use its heat. Wind, water and biomass energy are really forms of solar energy too. Unlike fossil fuels, these sources never run out and pollute far less.

ਪ੍ਰਮਾਣੂ ਦੇ ਅੰਦਰ ਦੀ ਸ਼ਕਤੀThe Power Inside the Atom

ਪਰਮਾਣੂ ਦੇ ਨਿਊਕਲੀਅਸ ਵਿੱਚ ਬੇਹਿਸਾਬ ਊਰਜਾ ਲੁਕੀ ਹੁੰਦੀ ਹੈ। ਜਦੋਂ ਯੂਰੇਨੀਅਮ ਵਰਗੇ ਭਾਰੀ ਪਰਮਾਣੂ ਦਾ ਨਿਊਕਲੀਅਸ ਟੁੱਟਦਾ ਹੈ — ਇਸ ਨੂੰ ਨਿਊਕਲੀ ਵਿਖੰਡਨ (fission) ਕਹਿੰਦੇ ਹਨ — ਤਾਂ ਭਾਰੀ ਮਾਤਰਾ ਵਿੱਚ ਗਰਮੀ ਨਿਕਲਦੀ ਹੈ, ਜੋ ਬਿਜਲੀ ਬਣਾਉਣ ਲਈ ਵਰਤੀ ਜਾਂਦੀ ਹੈ। ਥੋੜ੍ਹਾ ਜਿਹਾ ਬਾਲਣ ਬਹੁਤ ਸਾਰੀ ਊਰਜਾ ਦਿੰਦਾ ਹੈ, ਪਰ ਇਸ ਦੇ ਵੱਡੇ ਖ਼ਤਰੇ ਵੀ ਹਨ: ਰੇਡੀਓਐਕਟਿਵ ਕਚਰਾ ਹਜ਼ਾਰਾਂ ਸਾਲ ਖ਼ਤਰਨਾਕ ਰਹਿੰਦਾ ਹੈ ਤੇ ਕਿਸੇ ਦੁਰਘਟਨਾ ਦੇ ਗੰਭੀਰ ਸਿੱਟੇ ਹੋ ਸਕਦੇ ਹਨ।

Immense energy is locked inside an atom's nucleus. When the nucleus of a heavy atom like uranium is split — called nuclear fission — a huge amount of heat is released and used to make electricity. A tiny amount of fuel gives enormous energy, but there are serious risks: radioactive waste stays dangerous for thousands of years, and an accident can have grave consequences.

A Good Source of Energy

A useful energy source should supply large energy, be easy to store and transport, be economical, and cause minimum pollution.

High Output
Produces large amount of energy per unit mass or volume.
Easy Handling
Can be stored, transported and used safely.
Low Pollution
Produces little smoke, ash or greenhouse gas.

Conventional vs Non-conventional Sources

ConventionalNon-conventional
Used traditionally for a long time.Developed as alternatives to fossil fuels.
Coal, petroleum, natural gas, hydropower, biomass.Solar, wind, tidal, wave, geothermal, nuclear.
Often reliable but many cause pollution.Cleaner but may depend on location/weather.

Fossil Fuels

Coal, petroleum and natural gas formed from ancient plants and animals buried under the earth for millions of years.

  • They are concentrated energy stores.
  • They are non-renewable on human time scales.
  • Burning them releases CO₂, SO₂, NOx and smoke.
Fossil fuels are convenient but finite and polluting.

Thermal Power Plant

Most conventional electricity is produced by burning coal or gas to boil water.

1. Fuel burns and releases heat.
2. Heat converts water into high-pressure steam.
3. Steam spins a turbine.
4. Generator converts mechanical energy to electrical energy.
5. Electricity is sent through transmission lines.

Limitations of Fossil Fuels

  • Air pollution: smoke, ash, oxides of sulphur and nitrogen.
  • Acid rain: SO₂ and NOx dissolve in rainwater.
  • Global warming: CO₂ traps heat.
  • Finite supply: new fossil fuels take millions of years to form.

Improving Fuel Efficiency

Better design can reduce waste and pollution.

Hydro Power Plants

Water stored high in a dam has potential energy. When it falls, it turns turbines and generates electricity.

Potential energy of water → Kinetic energy → Mechanical energy → Electrical energy
Hydropower is renewable, but large dams can create serious social and environmental problems.

Environmental Problems of Dams

TypeProblem

Biomass

Biomass includes wood, crop residue, cow dung and other biological waste.

  • Direct burning is smoky and inefficient.
  • Controlled anaerobic digestion makes biogas.
  • Biomass is renewable if plants and animals are replenished.

Biogas Plant

Cow dung and plant waste decompose without oxygen to produce a methane-rich fuel.

PartFunction
Inlet tankDung slurry enters.
DigesterAnaerobic microbes produce biogas.
Gas tankCollects methane-rich gas.
OutletSpent slurry comes out as manure.
Biogas burns cleanly and the slurry is excellent fertiliser.

Wind Energy

Wind turbines convert kinetic energy of moving air into electrical energy.

  • Works best in coastal and open windy areas.
  • No fuel cost and no air pollution during operation.
  • Needs large land area and reliable wind speed.

Solar Energy

The sun is the ultimate source of most energy on Earth. Solar energy can be used directly as heat or converted into electricity.

  • Solar cookers and heaters use heat.
  • Solar cells convert light directly into electricity.
  • Solar panels have no moving parts and low running cost.

Solar Cooker

FeaturePurpose
Black surfaceAbsorbs more heat.
Glass coverTraps heat by greenhouse effect.
Plane mirrorReflects extra sunlight into the box.
Limitation: does not work well at night, in rain, or for frying.

Solar Cells

Solar cells use the photovoltaic effect to convert sunlight into electricity.

  • Used in calculators, satellites, traffic lights and remote areas.
  • Silicon cells are expensive to manufacture.
  • Panels need large area for high output.

Geothermal Energy

In some regions, rocks below the surface are very hot. Water reaching these hot rocks becomes steam that can turn turbines.

  • Useful near hot springs and volcanic regions.
  • Reliable day and night.
  • Limited to suitable geological locations.

Nuclear Energy

In nuclear fission, a heavy nucleus such as uranium splits and releases enormous energy.

AdvantageConcern
Very high energy output from small fuel mass.Radioactive waste must be stored safely.
Low CO₂ during operation.Accidents can be dangerous.

Hydrogen as a Fuel

Hydrogen has high calorific value and produces water on combustion.

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O + Energy
  • Clean product: water.
  • Storage and transport are difficult.
  • Producing hydrogen may require energy.

Local Decision Scenario

A remote village needs electricity for lights, phones and a water pump.

  • Sunny region: solar panels with battery backup.
  • Windy coastal region: wind turbines plus grid support.
  • Farming village: biogas for cooking and manure.
Best answer: use a mixed energy system based on local conditions.

Chapter 14 Recap

  • Fossil fuels are finite and polluting.
  • Thermal plants convert heat to mechanical to electrical energy.
  • Biogas is mostly methane and burns cleanly.
  • Solar cookers use black surfaces, mirrors and glass covers.
  • Dams produce electricity but can displace people.
  • No single energy source is perfect; choose by local conditions.

Introduction to Coordination

Why Respond?
All living organisms—whether it is a sunflower turning toward the morning sun or a human pulling their hand away from a hot surface—must be able to detect changes in their environment. These environmental changes are called stimuli. To survive, a multicellular organism must ensure that its millions of cells work together harmoniously to produce the correct response. This synchronized working of various organs is what we call Control and Coordination.

Structure of a Neuron (ਨਾੜੀ ਸੈੱਲ)

  1. Dendrite: Branch-like structures that acquire information.
  2. Cell Body (Soma): Processes the signal.
  3. Axon: Transmits the electrical impulse away from the cell body.
  4. Nerve Ending: The tip where the signal is passed on.

The Synapse Conversion (ਸਿਨੈਪਸ)

The gap between two neurons is called the synapse. Because signals cannot physically jump across this gap, the nerve ending converts the signal by releasing chemicals called . These chemicals rapidly diffuse across the gap and bind to the of the next neuron. This chemical conversion ensures that the impulse only flows in a strict direction.

The Reflex Arc Sequence 🏆 5-Mark Favorite

Interactive Sequencer: Build the chronological sequence of a reflex action.

1. Receptor: Thermoreceptors in your skin detect extreme heat.
2. Sensory Neuron: Rushes the pain signal up your arm towards the central nervous system.
3. Relay Neuron: Located strictly in the Spinal Cord. It instantly processes and redirects the signal without waiting for brain input.
4. Motor Neuron: Rushes the command back down your arm.
5. Effector: Your arm muscle contracts, pulling your hand away instantly!

Why Reflexes Skip the Brain (ਰੀੜ੍ਹ ਦੀ ਹੱਡੀ)

When you touch something hot, waiting for the brain to think would waste precious time and you could get burnt. So the spinal cord takes a shortcut.

  • The relay neuron in the spinal cord processes the signal instantly.
  • The command to move is sent before the brain even feels the pain.
  • This ready-made pathway is called the reflex arc.
Reflex actions are fast, automatic and are managed by the spinal cord, not the brain. ਪ੍ਰਤੀਵਰਤੀ ਕਿਰਿਆਵਾਂ ਰੀੜ੍ਹ ਦੀ ਹੱਡੀ ਦੁਆਰਾ ਕੰਟਰੋਲ ਹੁੰਦੀਆਂ ਹਨ।

How Do Muscles Move? (ਮਾਸਪੇਸ਼ੀਆਂ ਦੀ ਹਰਕਤ)

A nerve impulse alone is only a message — something must physically act on it. That job belongs to muscle cells.

  • Muscle cells contain special proteins that can change their shape and arrangement.
  • When a nerve impulse arrives, these proteins rearrange so the cell becomes shorter.
  • Shorter cells mean the muscle contracts, pulling the bone and causing movement.
Muscles can only PULL (contract), they cannot push. That is why they work in pairs. ਮਾਸਪੇਸ਼ੀਆਂ ਸਿਰਫ਼ ਖਿੱਚ ਸਕਦੀਆਂ ਹਨ, ਧੱਕ ਨਹੀਂ ਸਕਦੀਆਂ।

The Endocrine System

The Speed Limit of Nerves
Nervous tissue allows for incredibly rapid responses, but it has limitations. Firstly, electrical impulses only reach cells that are directly connected by nerve fibers. Secondly, once an impulse is sent, a neuron takes time to reset its mechanism before it can fire again. To provide a steady, slow, and wide-reaching coordination across the entire body (like regulating growth or puberty), we rely on the Endocrine System. This system uses chemical messengers called hormones, which are secreted directly into the bloodstream to reach every single cell.

The Insulin Feedback Loop (ਫੀਡਬੈਕ ਵਿਧੀ)

Hormones operate on a precise feedback mechanism to maintain homeostasis.

Step 1: You eat a meal → Blood sugar levels rise.
Step 2: The Pancreas detects the high sugar and secretes Insulin.
Step 3: Insulin commands cells to absorb sugar → Blood sugar levels fall.
Step 4: Pancreas detects the low sugar and stops/reduces insulin secretion to prevent fainting.

Thyroid, Iodine & Goitre (ਗਿਲੜ ਰੋਗ)

The thyroid gland makes the hormone thyroxine, which controls how fast the body uses carbohydrates, proteins and fats (its metabolism).

  • Thyroxine can only be made if the diet contains enough iodine.
  • A lack of iodine causes a swollen neck disease called goitre.
  • This is why we use iodised salt in cooking.
No iodine → No thyroxine → Goitre. ਆਇਓਡੀਨ ਦੀ ਕਮੀ ਨਾਲ ਗਿਲੜ ਰੋਗ ਹੁੰਦਾ ਹੈ।

When you are scared or angry, the adrenal glands release adrenaline straight into the blood to prepare you for quick action.

Plant Coordination

Slower, But Smart
Unlike animals, plants do not have a nervous system or muscles. They cannot run away from danger or physically grab nutrients. Instead, they coordinate their behavior entirely through Phytohormones (plant hormones). When a plant detects sunlight, gravity, or water, it uses these slow-moving chemicals to alter the growth rate of specific cells, allowing the stem to slowly bend towards light or the roots to seek out deep water.

ਐਲੂਮੀਨੀਅਮ ਦੀ ਢਾਲThe Anodising Shield

ਐਲੂਮੀਨੀਅਮ ਇੱਕ ਦਿਲਚਸਪ ਧਾਤ ਹੈ। ਰਸਾਇਣਕ ਤੌਰ 'ਤੇ ਇਹ ਬਹੁਤ ਕਿਰਿਆਸ਼ੀਲ ਹੈ ਤੇ ਜਲਦੀ ਖ਼ਰਾਬ ਹੋਣੀ ਚਾਹੀਦੀ, ਪਰ ਇਸ ਕੋਲ ਗੁਪਤ ਹਥਿਆਰ ਹੈ। ਹਵਾ ਦੇ ਸੰਪਰਕ ਵਿੱਚ ਆਉਂਦੇ ਹੀ ਇਸ ਉੱਤੇ ਐਲੂਮੀਨੀਅਮ ਆਕਸਾਈਡ ਦੀ ਪਾਰਦਰਸ਼ੀ, ਪਤਲੀ ਪਰਤ ਬਣ ਜਾਂਦੀ ਹੈ। ਲੋਹੇ ਦੀ ਜੰਗ ਵਾਂਗ ਇਹ ਝੜਦੀ ਨਹੀਂ, ਸਗੋਂ ਸਤ੍ਹਾ ਨਾਲ ਚਿਪਕੀ ਰਹਿ ਕੇ ਹੋਰ ਆਕਸੀਜਨ ਨੂੰ ਰੋਕਦੀ ਹੈ। ਇੰਜੀਨੀਅਰ ਬਿਜਲੀ ਨਾਲ ਇਸ ਪਰਤ ਨੂੰ ਹੋਰ ਮੋਟਾ ਬਣਾਉਂਦੇ ਹਨ — ਇਸ ਨੂੰ ਐਨੋਡਾਈਜ਼ਿੰਗ ਕਹਿੰਦੇ ਹਨ।

Aluminium is fascinating. By logic it is very reactive and should corrode fast, but it has a secret weapon. The moment it meets air, a thin, transparent skin of aluminium oxide forms. Unlike iron rust, this skin does not flake off; it sticks tightly and blocks further oxygen. Engineers deliberately thicken this oxide layer using electricity, a process called anodising, making aluminium nearly weatherproof.

ਥਰਮਿਟ ਵੈਲਡਿੰਗThe Thermit Welding Process

ਥਰਮਿਟ ਕਿਰਿਆ ਸਭ ਤੋਂ ਸ਼ਾਨਦਾਰ ਵਿਸਥਾਪਨ ਕਿਰਿਆਵਾਂ ਵਿੱਚੋਂ ਇੱਕ ਹੈ। ਜਦੋਂ ਆਇਰਨ(III) ਆਕਸਾਈਡ ਤੇ ਐਲੂਮੀਨੀਅਮ ਪਾਊਡਰ ਦੇ ਮਿਸ਼ਰਣ ਨੂੰ ਜਲਾਇਆ ਜਾਂਦਾ ਹੈ, ਤਾਂ ਵਧੇਰੇ ਕਿਰਿਆਸ਼ੀਲ ਐਲੂਮੀਨੀਅਮ ਲੋਹੇ ਨੂੰ ਉਸ ਦੇ ਆਕਸਾਈਡ ਤੋਂ ਹਟਾ ਦਿੰਦਾ ਹੈ। ਇਹ ਕਿਰਿਆ ਇੰਨੀ ਤਾਪ ਨਿਕਾਸੀ ਹੈ ਕਿ ਪੈਦਾ ਹੋਇਆ ਲੋਹਾ ਪਿਘਲ ਕੇ ਤਰਲ ਬਣ ਜਾਂਦਾ ਹੈ। ਇੰਜੀਨੀਅਰ ਇਸੇ ਪਿਘਲੇ ਲੋਹੇ ਨੂੰ ਟੁੱਟੀਆਂ ਰੇਲ ਪਟੜੀਆਂ ਦੇ ਜੋੜਾਂ ਵਿੱਚ ਵਹਾ ਕੇ ਉਨ੍ਹਾਂ ਨੂੰ ਜੋੜਦੇ ਹਨ।

The thermit reaction is one of the most spectacular displacement reactions. When iron(III) oxide and aluminium powder are ignited, the more reactive aluminium displaces iron from its oxide. The reaction is so exothermic that the iron produced is molten. Engineers pour this liquid iron into gaps in broken railway tracks to weld the heavy steel rails together seamlessly.

ਕਾਂਸੀ ਯੁੱਗ ਦੀ ਸਵੇਰThe Dawn of the Bronze Age

ਸ਼ੁੱਧ ਤਾਂਬਾ ਸੋਹਣਾ ਪਰ ਹਥਿਆਰ ਬਣਾਉਣ ਲਈ ਬਹੁਤ ਨਰਮ ਹੁੰਦਾ ਹੈ। ਹਜ਼ਾਰਾਂ ਸਾਲ ਪਹਿਲਾਂ ਧਾਤੂ-ਕਾਰੀਗਰਾਂ ਨੇ ਇੱਕ ਯੁੱਗ-ਪਲਟ ਖੋਜ ਕੀਤੀ: ਤਾਂਬੇ ਵਿੱਚ ਥੋੜ੍ਹਾ ਟੀਨ ਮਿਲਾ ਕੇ ਉਨ੍ਹਾਂ ਨੇ ਇੱਕ ਨਵਾਂ ਪਦਾਰਥ ਬਣਾਇਆ। ਦੋ ਧਾਤਾਂ ਦੇ ਇਸ ਮਿਸ਼ਰਣ ਨੂੰ ਮਿਸ਼ਰਧਾਤ (alloy) ਕਹਿੰਦੇ ਹਨ ਤੇ ਇਹ ਦੋਵਾਂ ਤੋਂ ਵਧੇਰੇ ਸਖ਼ਤ ਤੇ ਟਿਕਾਊ ਸੀ। ਇਸ ਨੂੰ ਕਾਂਸੀ (Bronze) ਕਿਹਾ ਗਿਆ, ਜਿਸ ਨੇ ਮਜ਼ਬੂਤ ਹਲ ਤੇ ਤਿੱਖੀਆਂ ਤਲਵਾਰਾਂ ਬਣਾ ਕੇ ਸੱਭਿਅਤਾਵਾਂ ਬਦਲ ਦਿੱਤੀਆਂ।

Pure copper is beautiful but far too soft for strong tools. Thousands of years ago metalworkers made a world-changing discovery: melting copper with a little tin created a new material. This mixture of metals, called an alloy, was harder and more durable than either parent metal. They named it Bronze, and it built stronger ploughs, sharper swords and mighty empires — the Bronze Age.

The Exceptions Rulebook

The board loves testing the exceptions to physical properties!

  • Liquid Metal: All metals are solid EXCEPT Mercury ($Hg$).
  • Soft Metals: Metals are hard, EXCEPT Sodium ($Na$), Potassium ($K$), and Lithium ($Li$) which can be cut with a knife.
  • Lustrous Non-metal: Non-metals are dull, EXCEPT Iodine ($I$).
  • Conducting Non-metal: Non-metals don't conduct electricity, EXCEPT Graphite (an allotrope of Carbon).
  • Low Melting Point Metals: Gallium ($Ga$) and Cesium ($Cs$) will melt if you just hold them in your palm!

Anodizing Aluminum

Aluminum is a highly reactive metal, but it is highly resistant to corrosion. Why?

When exposed to air, it naturally forms a thin oxide layer ($Al_2O_3$). This layer protects the metal underneath from further reaction.

📋 What is Anodizing?

Anodizing is a process of deliberately making this aluminum oxide layer thicker. A clean aluminum article is made the anode and electrolyzed with dilute sulphuric acid. The oxygen gas evolved at the anode reacts with aluminum to make a thicker protective oxide coat.

Ionic Bonds (ਆਇਨੀ ਬੰਧਨ)

Metals transfer electrons to non-metals. Example (NaCl): Sodium loses 1 electron (Na⁺), Chlorine gains 1 electron (Cl⁻).

  • Melting Points: Very high (ਉੱਚ ਪਿਘਲਣ ਅੰਕ) because of strong forces of electrostatic attraction.
  • Conduction: Conduct electricity in liquid solution (or molten state), but NOT in solid state because the ions are locked in place.

Metals Reacting with Water

Metals react differently based on their reactivity.

  • Cold Water (ਠੰਡਾ ਪਾਣੀ): Sodium ($Na$) and Potassium ($K$) react violently. The reaction is so exothermic the evolved hydrogen catches fire! Calcium reacts less violently and floats.
  • Hot Water (ਗਰਮ ਪਾਣੀ): Magnesium ($Mg$) reacts gently and floats.
  • Steam Only (ਸਿਰਫ਼ ਭਾਫ਼): Aluminum ($Al$), Iron ($Fe$), and Zinc ($Zn$). They don't react with liquid water at all.
  • No Reaction: Lead, Copper, Silver, Gold.

Metals Reacting with Acids (ਤੇਜ਼ਾਬਾਂ ਨਾਲ ਧਾਤਾਂ ਦੀ ਕਿਰਿਆ)

Generally: Metal + Dilute Acid → Salt + Hydrogen Gas

🚨 The Nitric Acid Anomaly

Hydrogen gas is NOT evolved when a metal reacts with Nitric Acid ($HNO_3$).

Why? $HNO_3$ is a strong oxidizing agent. It oxidizes the $H_2$ produced to water ($H_2O$) and itself gets reduced to nitrogen oxides (like $NO_2$ or $NO$).

(Only Magnesium and Manganese react with very dilute $HNO_3$ to give $H_2$ gas.)

Metallurgy: The 3 Stages

Minerals are compounds naturally found in the earth. If a mineral contains a very high percentage of metal that can be extracted profitably, it is an Ore. Dirt and sand mixed in are called Gangue.

  1. Enrichment of Ore: Removing the gangue using physical or chemical separation methods.
  2. Extraction of Metal: Getting the raw metal out of the ore (depends on reactivity).
  3. Refining: Purifying the extracted metal.

Extraction Based on Reactivity

  • Low Reactivity (e.g., Mercury, Copper): Can be reduced to metals by heating alone in air.
    2HgS + 3O₂ → 2HgO + 2SO₂ (then heat HgO to get Hg)
  • Medium Reactivity (e.g., Zinc, Iron): First converted to oxides (by roasting/calcination), then reduced using Carbon (coke).
  • High Reactivity (e.g., Sodium, Aluminum): Cannot be reduced by carbon. They are extracted by Electrolytic Reduction (electricity passed through molten ore).

Extraction Deep Dive

The Thermit Reaction (ਥਰਮਿਟ ਕਿਰਿਆ)

Highly exothermic displacement reaction. Iron(III) oxide + Aluminum makes molten iron. Used to join railway tracks!

Fe₂O₃ + 2Al → 2Fe(liquid) + Al₂O₃ + Heat

Electrolytic Refining (ਬਿਜਲਈ ਸੋਧਣ)

  • Anode (+): Impure copper block.
  • Cathode (-): Pure copper strip. Pure metal deposits here!
  • Anode Mud: Insoluble impurities that settle at the bottom of the anode.

🚨 Board Exam Pitfall

Do not reverse Anode and Cathode! Remember: The pure metal is collected at the negative terminal (Cathode).

Rusting & Alloys (ਜੰਗ ਲੱਗਣਾ)

Iron needs both Oxygen + Water to rust.

We prevent it by painting, oiling, or Galvanisation (ਗੈਲਵੈਨੀਕਰਨ) - coating steel/iron with a thin layer of Zinc.

Alloys (ਮਿਸ਼ਰਤ ਧਾਤਾਂ)

A homogeneous mixture of two or more metals. It improves properties (e.g., pure iron is too soft; adding carbon makes it hard steel).

  • Brass: Copper + Zinc
  • Bronze: Copper + Tin
  • Solder: Lead + Tin (low melting point for welding wires)
  • Amalgam: Any alloy where one of the metals is Mercury ($Hg$).

Electrolytic Refining of Metals (ਬਿਜਲੀ-ਵਿਭਾਜਨ ਦੁਆਰਾ ਸ਼ੁੱਧੀਕਰਨ)

The final purification step in metallurgy. Impure copper is purified using electrolysis:

  • Anode (+): thick block of impure metal — it dissolves.
  • Cathode (−): thin strip of pure metal — pure metal deposits here.
  • Electrolyte: solution of a salt of the same metal (e.g. CuSO₄).
  • Anode mud: insoluble impurities (Ag, Au) settle below the anode — a valuable by-product!

🚨 Exam Tip

Pure metal always goes to the cathode. Remember "reduction at cathode" — the metal ion gains electrons.

Preventing Corrosion (ਖੋਰ ਤੋਂ ਬਚਾਅ)

Corrosion (rusting of iron) needs both air (O₂) and moisture. Break that contact to prevent it:

MethodHow it protects
Painting / greasing / oilingForms a barrier that keeps out air & water
GalvanisationCoating iron with a layer of zinc
ElectroplatingDepositing a less reactive metal (e.g. Cr, Ni)
Alloyinge.g. iron + chromium + nickel → stainless steel
Galvanised iron resists rust even if the zinc scratches off — zinc is more reactive and is oxidised first (sacrificial protection). (ਜ਼ਿੰਕ ਪਹਿਲਾਂ ਖੋਰਦਾ ਹੈ।)

Chapter 3 Quick Recap

  • Metals are lustrous, malleable, ductile, and good conductors.
  • Non-metals are generally poor conductors and brittle.
  • Ionic compounds have high melting points and conduct in solution.
  • Reactivity series dictates displacement capabilities.
  • Metallurgy extracts metals via roasting, calcination, and electrolysis.

ਵਿਰਾਸਤ ਦੇ ਲੁਕੇ ਕਾਰਕHeredity's Hidden Factors

1860 ਦੇ ਦਹਾਕੇ ਵਿੱਚ ਇੱਕ ਮੱਠ ਦੇ ਸ਼ਾਂਤ ਬਗ਼ੀਚੇ ਵਿੱਚ ਗ੍ਰੇਗਰ ਮੈਂਡਲ ਨਾਂ ਦੇ ਸੰਤ ਨੇ ਸੱਤ ਸਾਲ ਹਜ਼ਾਰਾਂ ਮਟਰ ਦੇ ਪੌਦੇ ਉਗਾ ਕੇ ਗਿਣੇ। ਉਸ ਨੇ ਵੇਖਿਆ ਕਿ ਲੰਬੇ ਤੇ ਮਧਰੇ ਪੌਦੇ ਦੇ ਮੇਲ ਤੋਂ ਸਾਰੀ ਸੰਤਾਨ ਲੰਬੀ ਨਿਕਲੀ — ਮਧਰਾਪਣ ਲੁਪਤ ਹੋ ਗਿਆ। ਪਰ ਉਨ੍ਹਾਂ ਲੰਬੇ ਪੌਦਿਆਂ ਦੇ ਆਪਸੀ ਮੇਲ ਤੋਂ ਹਰ ਚਾਰ ਵਿੱਚੋਂ ਲਗਭਗ ਇੱਕ ਪੌਦਾ ਮੁੜ ਮਧਰਾ ਨਿਕਲਿਆ। ਮੈਂਡਲ ਨੇ ਸਮਝਿਆ ਕਿ ਗੁਣ ਲੁਕੇ ਕਾਰਕਾਂ — ਜਿਨ੍ਹਾਂ ਨੂੰ ਅੱਜ ਅਸੀਂ ਜੀਨ ਕਹਿੰਦੇ ਹਾਂ — ਰਾਹੀਂ ਲੰਘਦੇ ਹਨ।

In the 1860s, in the quiet garden of a monastery, a monk named Gregor Mendel spent seven years growing and counting thousands of pea plants. He saw that crossing a tall plant with a short one gave offspring that were all tall — shortness seemed to vanish. Yet breeding those tall plants together brought the short trait back in about one plant in four. Mendel realised traits are carried by hidden factors, which we now call genes.

ਪ੍ਰਭਾਵੀ ਅਤੇ ਅਪ੍ਰਭਾਵੀDominant and Recessive

ਹਰ ਜੀਵ ਨੂੰ ਹਰ ਗੁਣ ਲਈ ਦੋ ਕਾਰਕ ਮਿਲਦੇ ਹਨ — ਇੱਕ ਮਾਂ ਤੋਂ, ਇੱਕ ਪਿਤਾ ਤੋਂ। ਕਦੇ-ਕਦੇ ਇੱਕ ਕਾਰਕ ਦੂਜੇ ਨੂੰ ਦਬਾ ਦਿੰਦਾ ਹੈ। ਜੋ ਗੁਣ ਦਿਖਾਈ ਦਿੰਦਾ ਹੈ, ਉਸ ਨੂੰ ਪ੍ਰਭਾਵੀ (dominant) ਕਹਿੰਦੇ ਹਨ, ਤੇ ਜੋ ਲੁਕਿਆ ਰਹਿੰਦਾ ਹੈ, ਉਸ ਨੂੰ ਅਪ੍ਰਭਾਵੀ (recessive)। ਮੈਂਡਲ ਦੇ ਲੰਬੇ (Tt) ਪੌਦੇ ਵਿੱਚ ਮਧਰੇਪਣ ਦਾ ਕਾਰਕ ਲੁਕਿਆ ਰਹਿੰਦਾ ਹੈ। ਦੋ ਅਜਿਹੇ ਪੌਦਿਆਂ ਦੇ ਮੇਲ ਤੋਂ ਅਗਲੀ ਪੀੜ੍ਹੀ ਵਿੱਚ ਲੰਬੇ ਤੇ ਮਧਰੇ ਦਾ ਅਨੁਪਾਤ 3:1 ਬਣਦਾ ਹੈ।

Every organism gets two factors for each trait — one from each parent. Sometimes one factor masks the other. The trait that shows is called dominant, and the one that stays hidden is recessive. In Mendel's tall (Tt) plant, the factor for shortness is hidden. Crossing two such plants gives a next generation with tall and short in a ratio of 3:1.

ਮੁੰਡਾ ਜਾਂ ਕੁੜੀ?Boy or Girl?

ਬੱਚਾ ਮੁੰਡਾ ਹੋਵੇਗਾ ਜਾਂ ਕੁੜੀ, ਇਹ ਲਿੰਗ ਗੁਣਸੂਤਰਾਂ ਉੱਤੇ ਨਿਰਭਰ ਕਰਦਾ ਹੈ। ਔਰਤਾਂ ਵਿੱਚ ਦੋ X ਗੁਣਸੂਤਰ (XX) ਹੁੰਦੇ ਹਨ ਤੇ ਮਰਦਾਂ ਵਿੱਚ ਇੱਕ X ਤੇ ਇੱਕ Y (XY)। ਮਾਂ ਹਮੇਸ਼ਾ X ਗੁਣਸੂਤਰ ਦਿੰਦੀ ਹੈ, ਪਰ ਪਿਤਾ X ਜਾਂ Y ਦੇ ਸਕਦਾ ਹੈ। ਜੇ ਸ਼ੁਕਰਾਣੂ X ਲਿਆਵੇ ਤਾਂ ਬੱਚਾ ਕੁੜੀ (XX), ਤੇ ਜੇ Y ਲਿਆਵੇ ਤਾਂ ਮੁੰਡਾ (XY) ਹੁੰਦਾ ਹੈ। ਇਸ ਲਈ ਬੱਚੇ ਦਾ ਲਿੰਗ ਪਿਤਾ ਵੱਲੋਂ ਤੈਅ ਹੁੰਦਾ ਹੈ, ਨਾ ਕਿ ਮਾਂ ਵੱਲੋਂ।

Whether a baby is a boy or a girl depends on the sex chromosomes. Females have two X chromosomes (XX) and males have one X and one Y (XY). The mother always gives an X, but the father can give either X or Y. If the sperm carries X the child is a girl (XX); if it carries Y the child is a boy (XY). So the child's sex is decided by the father, not the mother.

Heredity & Variation 🏆 Definition

Heredity is the transmission of characters (traits) from parents to their offspring. Variation is the differences that appear among the offspring and their parents.

Heredity (ਵੰਸ਼ਾਗਤੀ)Variation (ਭਿੰਨਤਾ)
Traits passed to the next generation.Differences between individuals.
Makes offspring resemble parents.Helps a species survive changing conditions (basis of evolution).

Not all characteristics can be passed to the next generation.

Why Mendel Chose Pea Plants

Gregor Mendel (the "Father of Genetics") picked the garden pea (Pisum sativum) for good reasons:

  • It has clear contrasting traits (tall/short, round/wrinkled).
  • It has a short life cycle and produces many seeds.
  • It can self-pollinate and also be cross-pollinated by hand.

Mendel's Three Laws 🏆 Very Important

1. Law of Dominance: In a hybrid (Tt), only the dominant trait (T = Tall) is seen; the recessive trait stays hidden.
2. Law of Segregation: The two alleles of a pair separate during gamete formation, so each gamete carries only one allele.
3. Law of Independent Assortment: Genes for different traits are inherited independently (shown by the 9:3:3:1 dihybrid ratio).

3. Mendel's Monohybrid Cross (One Trait)

Pure Tall (TT) × Pure Short (tt).

  1. Parent Generation (P): Pure Tall (TT) × Pure Short (tt).
  2. First Generation (F1): All offspring receive one 'T' and one 't' (Tt). Because 'T' is dominant, 100% of F1 plants are TALL. The short trait is hidden.
  3. Second Generation (F2): He self-pollinated the F1 plants (Tt × Tt).
    • The hidden short trait reappears! The final Phenotypic Ratio is 3 Tall : 1 Short.
Monohybrid Cross F2 Ratio = 3:1

4. Mendel's Dihybrid Cross (Two Traits)

A cross focusing on TWO traits simultaneously.
Example: Round/Yellow seeds (RRYY) × Wrinkled/Green seeds (rryy).

  • F1 Generation: All seeds are Round and Yellow (RrYy).
  • F2 Generation: New combinations appear! Some are Round/Green, some are Wrinkled/Yellow.
Dihybrid Cross F2 Ratio = 9:3:3:1
⚠️ Board Trap The 9:3:3:1 ratio proves the Law of Independent Assortment. It means traits do not influence each other (a seed's shape does not dictate its color).

Sex Determination in Humans 🏆 3-Mark Sequence

Interactive Sequencer: Why is the probability of a boy or girl exactly 50%?

1. The Base: Humans have 23 pairs of chromosomes. The 23rd pair are the sex chromosomes. Females are XX, Males are XY.
2. Gametes (Mothers): During egg formation, pairs separate. The mother produces eggs that ALL carry the X chromosome (100% X).
3. Gametes (Fathers): The father produces sperm where 50% carry X and 50% carry Y.
4. Fertilization: If an X-sperm fertilizes the egg → Girl (XX). If a Y-sperm fertilizes the egg → Boy (XY).
Conclusion: The mother always gives an X. The sex of the child is determined entirely by what the father contributes!

How Are Traits Expressed?

A gene is a piece of DNA that carries instructions to make a specific protein (enzyme). These proteins build the body and control its functions, producing the visible trait.

Gene (DNA) → Protein / Enzyme → Trait

Example: a working gene makes enough growth hormone → tall plant; a faulty gene makes less → short plant.

Dihybrid Cross: Building 9:3:3:1 🏆 5-Mark Derivation

Interactive Sequencer: Reveal each step to see how the famous F2 ratio appears. (9:3:3:1 ਅਨੁਪਾਤ ਕਿਵੇਂ ਬਣਦਾ ਹੈ, ਹਰ ਪੜਾਅ ਖੋਲ੍ਹੋ।)

1. Parents (P): Cross a pure Round-Yellow plant RRYY with a pure wrinkled-green plant rryy.
2. F1 Generation: All offspring are RrYy → 100% Round & Yellow (both dominant traits appear).
3. Gametes from F1: Each RrYy makes 4 gamete types — RY, Ry, rY, ry (Law of Independent Assortment).
4. F2 Punnett Grid: 4 gametes × 4 gametes = 16 boxes of offspring combinations.
5. Count the phenotypes: 9 Round-Yellow : 3 Round-green : 3 wrinkled-Yellow : 1 wrinkled-green.
Result: The ratio 9 : 3 : 3 : 1 proves genes for two traits are inherited independently.

Evolution: Darwin's Natural Selection (ਵਿਕਾਸ: ਕੁਦਰਤੀ ਚੋਣ)

Charles Darwin explained how species change over generations through Natural Selection — "survival of the fittest".

  • Variation: individuals in a population differ slightly.
  • Struggle for existence: more offspring are born than can survive.
  • Survival of the fittest: the best-adapted variations survive and reproduce.
  • Inheritance: useful traits are passed on, so the population slowly changes.
Nature "selects" the fittest variations — over long periods this produces new species. (ਕੁਦਰਤ ਸਭ ਤੋਂ ਯੋਗ ਨੂੰ ਚੁਣਦੀ ਹੈ।)

Speciation & Lamarck vs Darwin (ਪ੍ਰਜਾਤੀਕਰਨ)

Speciation is the formation of a new species when a group can no longer interbreed with the original population — often due to geographical isolation and genetic drift.

LamarckDarwin
Inheritance of acquired characters (use & disuse)Natural selection of inherited variations
Giraffe stretched its neck, passed it onLonger-necked giraffes survived & reproduced
Acquired traits (e.g. a cut, big muscles) are not inherited — only changes in germ (reproductive) cells pass on. (ਗ੍ਰਹਿਣ ਕੀਤੇ ਲੱਛਣ ਵਿਰਾਸਤ ਵਿੱਚ ਨਹੀਂ ਮਿਲਦੇ।)
